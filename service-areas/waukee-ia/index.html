The gutter guard system we install

We install a low-profile stainless steel micro-mesh screen that is designed to mount on your existing gutters and handle real Iowa storms, not just gentle drizzle in a brochure.

Why we like this style of guard

  • Stainless steel mesh will not warp, rot, or crumble like foam and plastic inserts.
  • Low-profile panel that works with existing 5" and 6" K-style gutters on most asphalt roofs.
  • Engineered to move real storm water, not just look clean in a brochure photo.
  • Installed after a full gutter tune-up so the system actually drains before it is covered.

Our installation process

Every job follows the same checklist so your gutters actually work, not just look good in a photo.

Step 1

Clean gutters & downspouts

We remove all leaves, needles, and debris from your gutters and blow out your downspouts to confirm they are flowing correctly.

Step 2

Check slope & fastening

We inspect for sagging sections, improper slopes, and loose hangers. If water won’t move, gutter guards won’t fix it.

Step 3

Install gutter guards

We install gutter guards designed to handle Iowa storms, fastening into the gutter and/or fascia per manufacturer specs.

Step 4

Final walkthrough

We walk the property with you, talk through any problem areas, and review what was done before we leave.

Will gutter guards work in heavy rain?

Yes. Our micro-mesh system is specifically designed to handle heavy Iowa downpours. The fine mesh allows water to sheet through while debris slides off the angled surface. In extreme rainfall, some water may overshoot (as with any gutter system), but the guards prevent the clogs that cause real water damage problems.

Do gutter guards cause ice dams in winter?

Properly installed gutter guards don't cause ice dams. Ice dams form due to heat escaping from your attic, not from gutter guards. Our low-profile guards actually allow snow to slide off more easily than open gutters packed with frozen debris. We install with proper spacing to ensure winter performance.
