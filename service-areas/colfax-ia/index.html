The gutter guard system we install

We install a low-profile stainless steel micro-mesh screen that is designed to mount on your existing gutters and handle real Iowa storms, not just gentle drizzle in a brochure.

Why we like this style of guard

  • Stainless steel mesh will not warp, rot, or crumble like foam and plastic inserts.
  • Low-profile panel that works with existing 5" and 6" K-style gutters on most asphalt roofs.
  • Engineered to move real storm water, not just look clean in a brochure photo.
  • Installed after a full gutter tune-up so the system actually drains before it is covered.

Our installation process

Every job follows the same checklist so your gutters actually work, not just look good in a photo.

Step 1

Clean gutters & downspouts

We remove all leaves, needles, and debris from your gutters and blow out your downspouts to confirm they are flowing correctly.

Step 2

Check slope & fastening

We inspect for sagging sections, improper slopes, and loose hangers. If water won’t move, gutter guards won’t fix it.

Step 3

Install gutter guards

We install gutter guards designed to handle Iowa storms, fastening into the gutter and/or fascia per manufacturer specs.

Step 4

Final walkthrough

We walk the property with you, talk through any problem areas, and review what was done before we leave.

What areas of Iowa do you serve?

We focus on Central Iowa communities within an easy drive of the Des Moines metro. That includes cities like Des Moines, West Des Moines, Ankeny, Altoona, Waukee, Ames, Pella, Newton, Grinnell, Oskaloosa, and many nearby towns. If you’re in Central Iowa, there’s a good chance you’re in our service area.

Do gutter guards mean I will never clean my gutters again?

No system is truly “never ever clean again,” but a good micro-mesh guard should drastically cut down on ladder trips. Most homeowners just hose the top of the guards off once in a while, or ask us to check things during future exterior work.

Can you install gutter guards on my existing gutters?

In most cases, yes. As long as your gutters are sized correctly, fastened well, and not rotted out, we can clean, tune, and then install guards on your existing system. If we spot sections that are too far gone, we’ll point them out and give you options.

What type of gutter guards do you install?

We install a stainless steel micro-mesh system with a rigid aluminum frame. It’s designed to handle Iowa storms, maple helicopters, pine needles, and roof grit without the foam, plastic, or hood-style problems you may have seen before.

How much does gutter guard installation cost?

Pricing depends on total gutter footage, number of stories, roof pitch, and how much repair or tuning is needed before we install guards. We price each project after looking at your home and provide a clear written estimate before any work starts.
